%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Data Science at the interserction among CS, AI, Statistics.

Prominent reliance on data-driven algorithms and big data processing facilities to attain high predictive performance.

It works very well as data is attained by sampling the reality, and DS let our algorithms adhere and adapt to that reality, in a way which is increasingly more precise as the amount of data increases.

Very effective and flexible aproach, yet characterised by a number of limitations concerning: the distributed representation of knowledge, lack of commonsense, lack of reasoning, opaqueness, and lack of intepretability. 

Computational Logic at the intersection among CS, Mathematics, and Logic.

Prominent reliance on symbolic representation of data, inference, and automated reasoning to attain sound results to well formalised problems.

Historically, it has boosted the early advacens of AI, following the purpose of letting machines attain human-like reasoing capabilities.

Very powerfull, and crisp approach, yet characterised by a number of limitations concerning: the provisioning of knowledge, lack of flexibility in the management of fuzzy/blurry scenarios, the scalability of learning, the computational time/space requirements of most advanced algorithms in this field.

It is quite straightforward to realise that the two disciplines are complementary on many sides.
